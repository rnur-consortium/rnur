# Reddit Neographical Unicode Registry (RNUR) - Code of Conduct

## 1. Our Pledge
As contributors, maintainers, and coordinators of the Reddit Neographical Unicode Registry (RNUR), we pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to acting and interacting in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## 2. Our Standards

### Expected Behavior
* **Respect Creative Ownership:** Acknowledge and respect that constructed scripts, languages, and font implementations represent significant individual creative effort. Always credit original creators when discussing or documenting allocations.
* **Technical Collaboration:** Focus on objective, constructive technical discussion regarding Unicode architecture, glyph metrics, script engineering, and allocation conflicts.
* **Inclusivity:** Use welcoming and inclusive language, and be respectful of differing viewpoints and technical approaches to script registry management.
* **Grace under Conflict:** Gracefully accept constructive criticism and respect community consensus when resolving overlapping allocation requests.
* **Empathy:** Show empathy and kindness towards other community members, recognizing that many contributors are hobbyists, students, and independent designers.

### Unacceptable Behavior
* **Plagiarism and Unauthorized Registration:** Attempting to claim, register, or lock down allocations for scripts or font projects created by others without their explicit consent.
* **Harassment and Trolling:** The use of sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome sexual attention or advances, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
* **Disruption:** Sustained disruption of registry discussions, technical arguments acting in bad faith, or intentional "camping" on Unicode code points without active development plans.
* **Publishing Private Information:** Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without explicit permission (doxing).
* **Unprofessional Conduct:** Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or collaborative open-source environment.

## 3. Enforcement Responsibilities
Registry maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate, fair, and corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for enforcement decisions when appropriate.

## 4. Scope
This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces (including the GitHub repository, registry documentation, and associated issue trackers), and it also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples of representing our project include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## 5. Enforcement
Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.
